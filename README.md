# User Retention & Growth Case Study (Synthetic SaaS Data)

## Overview
This project is an end-to-end data analytics case study based on a synthetic SaaS dataset. It covers user behavior, retention, revenue dynamics, and growth forecasting, with a focus on translating data into actionable business insights.

The goal was to simulate a real-world analytics scenario: supporting product and business decisions using data.

---

## Key Questions
- How is user adoption evolving over time?
- What drives retention and long-term value?
- Which customer segments are most valuable?
- Which onboarding approach performs better?
- What does future growth look like?

---

## Key Insights
- **Adoption is growing steadily** with clear seasonality; daily active users increased ~3x over the observed period.
- **Enterprise users drive the majority of value**, generating significantly higher long-term revenue per account.
- **Onboarding approach B outperforms A**, increasing early engagement by ~15% across typical users.
- **Growth is expected to continue**, with forecasted DAU reaching ~1,620 on average and up to ~1,950 in peak scenarios.

👉 See full presentation: [Results_Presentation.pptx](./Results_Presentation.pptx)

---

## Business Recommendations
- Prioritize **enterprise segment acquisition and retention**
- Roll out **onboarding approach B** across new users
- Plan infrastructure capacity with a **buffer above forecasted DAU**
- Monitor **segment mix and pricing changes** as key revenue drivers
